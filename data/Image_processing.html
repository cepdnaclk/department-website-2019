Image Processing Lab



Images are being used increasingly in industrial applications as the images can capture the details that other sensors cannot recognize. However the images are represented as a set of numbers and extracting features from an image is a complex process and hence giving interesting questions to the researches. The introduction of low cost high resolution video cameras forces the industry to use image processing in real time. This increases the complexity o image processing and challenging the ability of computer engineers to capture, filter and analyze streams of images in real time. Image Processing Research Group is working on areas of image analysis and feature extraction of individual frames and also in the areas of using of parallel and distributed processing at the processor level, system level and also at the cloud level computing platforms.
